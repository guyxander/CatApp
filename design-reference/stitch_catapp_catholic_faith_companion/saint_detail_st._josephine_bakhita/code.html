Life & History

Born in the Darfur region of Sudan in 1869, Josephine Bakhita was kidnapped by slave traders at the age of seven. Her name, "Bakhita," which means "fortunate," was given to her by her captors. For years, she endured the trauma of forced labor and physical suffering.

Eventually, she was purchased by an Italian diplomat who brought her to Italy. There, she was introduced to the Canossian Sisters and discovered the Catholic faith. For the first time, she encountered a "Master" who loved her—God. She was baptized in 1890 and later entered the religious life.

Known for her infectious smile and gentle spirit, she spent her life in Schio, Italy, serving her community and bearing witness to the healing power of forgiveness. She was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 2000.
